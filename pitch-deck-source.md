## P2: The Problem — Behavioral Data Is Broken

### Three layers. One root cause.

**Layer 1: Fitness data is unverifiable.**
- GPS can be spoofed. Steps can be shaken. Check-ins can be faked.
- 30-50% of fitness app "active user" data is noise.
- Insurers, employers, and researchers refuse to pay for unverifiable data.

**Layer 2: Behavioral data has no property rights.**
- You ran 5K today. Who owns that data record?
- Current answer: Apple. Keep. Google.
- Individuals cannot prove, trade, or monetize their own behavioral history.

**Layer 3: The insurance data market is stuck in a chicken-and-egg death loop.**
> Insurers need scaled behavioral data → Scale requires capital → Capital requires proof insurers will pay → Proof requires data. **Nobody has broken this loop.**

### The root cause:
**There is no verification layer for human physical behavior.**

The internet has TCP/IP. Payments have Visa. Physical behavior has nothing. Without a verification layer, behavioral data remains unpriceable, untradeable, and unowned by the people who generate it.

### Anchors:
- Global health data market: $500B (2026E)
- Effectively utilized behavioral data: <5%
- Fitness startups failing: 42% die from "building what nobody wanted" (Anthropic, 2026)
